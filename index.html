Rucking—walking with a weighted backpack—taps into our evolutionary roots. For hundreds of thousands of years, humans practiced persistence hunting, covering long distances on foot while carrying tools, food, and supplies. This combination of endurance and load-bearing movement is deeply encoded in our biology. Modern research even points to the role of the gravitostat—a weight-sensing mechanism in the body that may help regulate appetite and energy balance. By reintroducing this ancestral form of movement, rucking may support fat loss, muscle endurance, and metabolic health in a way that aligns with how our bodies evolved to move.

How This Calculator Works

Our advanced algorithm calculates:

  • Calories burned based on MET (Metabolic Equivalent) values adjusted for weight, terrain, and elevation
  • Pace analysis to help optimize your training

Optimizing Your Rucking Workouts

For best results:

  • Start with 10-15% of your body weight
  • Use proper military-style backpacks with hip belts
  • Maintain good posture - shoulders back, core engaged
  • Gradually increase weight and distance
